Departments of the Federal Investigation Bureau (FIB)

1. Criminal Investigations Division (CID): The CID is at the forefront of investigating and solving complex criminal cases. Our skilled detectives meticulously gather evidence, analyze data, and conduct interviews to bring perpetrators to justice. From financial crimes to homicides, CID is dedicated to uncovering the truth behind every crime.

2. Joint Terrorism Task Force (JTTF): The JTTF is a collaborative effort between FIB and other law enforcement agencies to combat terrorism and prevent potential threats. Working in coordination with local, state, and federal partners, JTTF agents gather intelligence and conduct operations to neutralize terrorist activities.

3. Cyber Crimes Unit (CCU): In the digital age, cybercrimes pose a significant threat to our society. The CCU is responsible for investigating cyber-related offenses, including hacking, online fraud, and identity theft. With cutting-edge technology and expertise, CCU agents ensure a safer cyberspace for all.

4. Intelligence and Analysis Division (IAD): The IAD gathers and analyzes intelligence from various sources to identify emerging threats and criminal trends. Through comprehensive analysis, IAD helps other divisions make informed decisions, contributing to our proactive approach in fighting crime.

5. Special Operations Division (SOD): SOD handles high-risk and sensitive operations that require precision and expertise. From undercover operations to hostage rescues, SOD agents are well-trained and equipped to handle critical situations with tact and professionalism.

6. Fugitive Apprehension Unit (FAU): The FAU is responsible for locating and apprehending fugitives on the run. With a relentless pursuit of justice, FAU agents track down individuals wanted for serious offenses and ensure they face the consequences of their actions.

Each of these departments plays a vital role in the FIB's commitment to protecting the citizens of Los Santos and maintaining a safer environment. Our dedicated agents work tirelessly to maintain the highest standards of excellence and integrity in their pursuit of justice.

Investigation Process:

  • Gathering Intelligence: FIB initiates investigations by gathering intelligence from various sources, including informants, surveillance, data analysis, and tips from the public.
  • Case Assignment: Once a case is identified, it is assigned to a specialized division within FIB based on the nature and complexity of the crime.
  • Evidence Collection: FIB agents meticulously collect evidence, including physical evidence, documents, and digital data, to build a strong case against the suspects.
  • Interviews and Interrogations: FIB agents conduct interviews and interrogations of witnesses, victims, and suspects to gather more information and establish the sequence of events.
  • Collaboration with Other Agencies: FIB often collaborates with other law enforcement agencies, both at the local and federal levels, to pool resources and expertise for complex cases or when dealing with criminals operating across jurisdictions.

Collaboration with NOOSE:

  • Combating Terrorism: Both FIB and NOOSE are integral parts of the United States' counter-terrorism efforts.
  • Joint Operations: FIB and NOOSE collaborate on joint operations to handle high-risk situations, such as hostage rescues or apprehending dangerous fugitives.
  • Information Sharing: FIB and NOOSE share critical information and intelligence on ongoing investigations and security matters.
  • Policy Development: FIB and NOOSE collaborate on the development of policies and strategies to address current and future security challenges.

Overall, the FIB's investigations are characterized by a systematic and evidence-based approach, with a strong focus on collaboration and intelligence-sharing. The partnership with NOOSE enhances the efficiency and effectiveness of both agencies in protecting the nation and its citizens from various threats.

The FIB's HR department plays a vital role in managing personnel, fostering a positive work environment, and ensuring the agency's workforce is well-supported and motivated. HR is responsible for various aspects, including recruitment, training, performance evaluation, benefits administration, and employee relations.

Recruitment

HR collaborates with hiring managers to identify staffing needs and posts job openings. They review applications, conduct interviews, and select candidates who possess the skills and qualifications required for FIB positions.

Training and Development

HR designs and delivers training programs to equip agents with the necessary skills and knowledge to excel in their roles. They also organize workshops and seminars to foster continuous learning and professional growth.

Performance Evaluation

HR conducts regular performance evaluations to assess employees' contributions and identify areas for improvement. Feedback from evaluations is used to support career development and promote excellence within the agency.

Employee Benefits

HR administers various employee benefits, such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid leave, to ensure the well-being and welfare of FIB agents.

Employee Relations

HR handles employee relations matters, ensuring a respectful and inclusive work environment. They address employee concerns, resolve conflicts, and promote a positive workplace culture.

The Joint Terrorism Task Force (JTTF) is a collaborative effort between the FIB and various law enforcement agencies in Los Santos. Its primary objective is to combat terrorism and prevent potential threats to national security. The JTTF brings together the expertise and resources of different agencies to identify, investigate, and neutralize terrorist activities.

Collaboration with NOOSE and Other Agencies

The FIB works closely with the National Office of Security Enforcement (NOOSE) and other local, state, and federal agencies in Los Santos to share intelligence and coordinate efforts. This collaboration allows for a more comprehensive approach to counter-terrorism, enabling the JTTF to respond swiftly and effectively to emerging threats.

Gathering and Analyzing Intelligence

The JTTF gathers and analyzes intelligence from various sources, including surveillance, informants, and open-source data. This information is shared with partner agencies, enhancing their understanding of potential threats and enabling coordinated action.

Coordinated Operations

When a potential threat is identified, the JTTF plans and executes joint operations with partner agencies. These operations often involve sharing resources, expertise, and tactical support to ensure successful outcomes.

Preventing Terrorism

Through its proactive approach, the JTTF aims to prevent terrorist attacks before they occur. By monitoring suspicious activities and individuals, the task force can intervene and disrupt terrorist plans.

Protecting the Public

The JTTF's collaboration with other agencies ensures a unified response to protect the public and maintain the safety and security of the city of Los Santos.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: How can I apply to become an FIB agent?

A: To apply, visit our Careers page and submit your application through the online portal. Ensure you meet the eligibility criteria and provide all required documents.

Q: What are the requirements to join the FIB?

A: Applicants must be at least 21 years old, have a clean criminal record, and meet specific education and experience criteria. Check our Careers page for detailed requirements.

Q: Does the FIB offer training for new recruits?

A: Yes, the FIB provides comprehensive training programs for new recruits to equip them with the necessary skills and knowledge for their roles.

Q: How can I report suspicious activities to the FIB?

A: If you notice any suspicious activities related to national security or terrorism, contact our dedicated hotline at [phone number] or submit a tip anonymously through our website.

Q: Does the FIB work with other law enforcement agencies?

A: Yes, the FIB collaborates with various local, state, and federal agencies, including NOOSE, to address security challenges and combat terrorism.

Q: What is the role of the FIB's Joint Terrorism Task Force (JTTF)?
